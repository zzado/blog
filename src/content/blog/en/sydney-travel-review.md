## Travel Spots

### 1. Watsons Bay üåÖ

The place I still see when I close my eyes.

![Watsons Bay at sunset](/blog/images/blog/sydney/watsons-bay-1.jpg)

Most people visit during the day for the coastal walk, but we overslept and ended up taking the ferry at **sunset**. Turned out to be the best decision.

![Watsons Bay park](/blog/images/blog/sydney/watsons-bay-2.jpg)

The sunset, the harbor, the towering trees ‚Äî it looked like a scene from a storybook. Daytime is beautiful too, but Watsons Bay at dusk is something else entirely.

> üí° There's a fish and chips shop there. It's... fine. Just okay.

### 2. Sydney Observatory

They say every Korean tourist in Sydney ends up here, and it does feel that way. The Harbour Bridge and Opera House views are famous, but **the real recommendation is to walk deeper inside**.

![Sunset from the Observatory](/blog/images/blog/sydney/observatory-sunset.jpg)

You'll find a quiet, open field where you can see the western sky. The sunset views are breathtaking ‚Äî worth more than the postcard harbor shots.

### 3. Ferry üö¢

The most memorable moment of the entire trip was **standing at the bow of a ferry heading toward Circular Quay**.

- The seats are comfortable, but **stand at the bow and face forward** ‚Äî feel the wind and the speed
- That rush of cutting through the water is indescribable
- Note: each ferry has a different layout ‚Äî some don't allow access to the front

### 4. Hyde Park & Royal Botanic Gardens üåø

Two words for both: **bring a mat.**

Hyde Park had a saxophone player, and at the Botanic Gardens, we spent two hours lying on the grass doing absolutely nothing. The gardens are enormous ‚Äî aim for **Fleet Steps** and walk from there until the Opera House view suddenly opens up on a wide lawn.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è The Botanic Gardens **close at 4 PM** (varies by season ‚Äî check first). Security starts clearing people around 3:40 PM. Follow the crowd to the exit ‚Äî the entrance you came in through might be locked!

### 5. Honestly... Everything Was Great

Every corner of Sydney was worth visiting. The harbor at Circular Quay, the alleyways of The Rocks, the waves at Bondi Beach... the entire city is one big travel spot.

## Tours

### Featherdale Wildlife Park + Blue Mountains

We did just one tour during the entire trip. Sydney's city center alone has so much to see that time runs short quickly.

- **Featherdale Wildlife Park** ‚Äî hold a koala for photos (extra ~$15 AUD) and feed kangaroos. We debated including this and are so glad we did
- **Blue Mountains** ‚Äî highly recommended, but bring **hand warmers** ‚Äî it gets seriously cold
- At night, the Milky Way was spectacularly clear ‚Äî the guide said it was one of the top 3 nights they'd seen

## Tips

### Weather

If weather forecasts are stressing out your planning, here's what worked for us:

- A week before departure, it showed rain all week. **Two days before, it suddenly changed to sunny**
- **The forecast from the day before departure is the most accurate**
- Strategy: plan *where* you want to go, but **don't fix dates** ‚Äî decide on-site based on actual weather
- Use **BOM Weather** (Australia's official weather app) on location and book tours on the best day

### Useful Apps

![Sydney travel apps](/blog/images/blog/sydney/sydney-apps.jpg)

Install these before you leave:

- **Australian ETA** ‚Äî Electronic Travel Authority (required for entry)
- **Opal Travel** ‚Äî Sydney transit card app (ferry, bus, tram)
- **TripView Lite** ‚Äî Real-time public transport schedules
- **BOM Weather** ‚Äî Bureau of Meteorology official app
- **Uber / DiDi** ‚Äî Ride-hailing alternatives
- **Trip.com** ‚Äî Accommodation and flight booking
- **Travel Wallet** ‚Äî Currency exchange and payments

## Looking Back

Nine days felt too short for everything Sydney has to offer. The sunset at Watsons Bay, the wind on the ferry, the lazy afternoon at the Botanic Gardens ‚Äî these are the moments that will stay with me.

What makes Sydney special is that nature is always within reach. One ferry ride from downtown and you're at a beach. A short walk and you're in a park. Don't just rush from spot to spot ‚Äî make time to sit and simply take in the scenery.

If I go back? I'm grabbing a Bar Luca burger, heading to the Observatory, and watching the sunset. That's mission number one for Sydney, round two.
